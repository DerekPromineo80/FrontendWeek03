In accounting, liabilities are financial obligations that a business or person owes to another party. They can be money, goods, or services.

See liabilities examples below.

Utilities Payable: These are the amounts owed to utility companies for services that have been used but not yet paid for. They are current liabilities because they are usually paid within a year. Utilities payable are shown on a company's balance sheet.

Taxes: income taxes, sales tax, capital gains tax, and other taxes owed to the government.

Wages: Employee compensation that has been earned but not yet paid.
