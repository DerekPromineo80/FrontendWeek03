In accounting, assets are resources that a business owns that can be converted to cash or used to generate income. Assets are listed on a company's balance sheet.

See asset examples below.

Real Estate: Land is an asset that can be owned by a company. It does not depreciate. You record the land's value at the cost of purchase. Even if the land's value goes up by the market value, the land is only recorded as having the value of the amount that it cost to purchase.

Equipment: is considered a long-term asset as it is used for multiple years to benefit a business. Equipment has a physical form, unlike intangible assets like patents or trademarks.

Material: typically classified as part of a company's inventory, which is considered a current asset. As the materials are used in the production process, their value is gradually transferred to the "work-in-progress" and then "finished goods" inventory categories.
