In accounting, equity in business is the portion of a company that its shareholders own. It can also refer to the amount of capital that shareholders receive if the company is sold or goes bankrupt.

See equity examples below.

Shareholders' equity: The equity that shareholders have in a company. This is common in corporations.

Owner's equity: The equity that a sole proprietor has in their business. This describes what the owner can claim if the business is sold.

Common stock: A type of equity that gives shareholders certain rights, such as voting in board elections.
